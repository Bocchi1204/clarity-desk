Linux Starter Pack

A practical resource page for people who want Linux to feel useful sooner, with less trial-and-error and less random setup noise.

Stay updatedRead related article

What this page is trying to solve

Many new Linux users do not actually need more “Linux philosophy.” They need a practical starting point: what to install first, what to configure early, what mistakes to avoid, and how to make the machine feel stable enough for real work.

What it includes

  • A first-week Linux setup checklist
  • An essential apps shortlist that avoids bloat
  • A practical VS Code setup section
  • Simple laptop maintenance habits
  • Common beginner mistakes to avoid

Who it is for

Students, everyday laptop users, and beginners who want a smoother first month on Linux without spending hours bouncing between forum threads and random tutorials.

Why it is worth having

The goal is not to overwhelm new users with options. The goal is to reduce friction, remove noise, and give people a more useful first setup.
